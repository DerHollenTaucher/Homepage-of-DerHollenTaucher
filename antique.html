Antique I Abyss of Time

As it’s my first time to visit here, it feels like a place I have been familiar with from the abyss of a thousand years, and another thousand years. And suddenly, there’s a feeling that I was born to get here, and to finish some tasks. It’s certainly a beautiful country! But it’s a bad luck that my landlord has no other chamber available for me. But I implored that just let me stay here, even in the loft, and she agreed. It’s an old wooden hostel from the eras of antique, the days of yore, and a thick layer of dust cloaked the floor. I can’t help sneezing after I get into it, and seems silver needles dancing under the slanting moonlight which streams in from the tiny window. I just wandered around without any certain purpose, with a candle ignited in my hand.

The tolling of the bell, which witnessed the night’s soundness, from somewhere so far, far away. I can hear some liquid was trickling from somewhere, with a viscous feeling. A sporadic wail echoes in the distance. I remembered that I was lying on the timber, and gusty wind flooded over my ears, and as my palms crossed on my chest, my finger closed on the fingers of a stiff, ice-cold hand. And there’s blood-red rain coming down to my bed, stained my face. With the meow of cats, a most melancholy voice sobbed “unchain my soul”; I tried to draw back my palm, but the little frozen palm just clung to it, the voice’s shivering “Finally and in the end, you’re back.” Thereat began an almost screaming voice “Let me out, let me out. I’ve been imprisoned here for millennia.” I tried to calm myself down “Who are you? What can I do?”

“You got the key, almost, almost!” she cried, buried in that leather trunk. “Got the key!” The voice came out from all directions. “Got the key!” Terror made me clap my hands over my ears and close my eyes. Then it’s a deathlike silence after a continuous moaning.

I struggled to open my eyes, and see an eerie dim of light come upstairs, with the oak floor cracking, my blood almost clotted, and thank god, it’s the landlord with a lantern. The dim lantern light reflected in her eyes, like flames ignited. At last, she said in a half-whisper way “Why you opened that leather trunk?” But it seems she didn’t expect any answer, she hesitated, and lapsed into silence. A little black cat's meow ultimately broke the tension.

“What are you doing? Lie your body down as before, and until another morning. But never scream like that, for god’s sake!” Then she murmured to herself while leaving this little chamber.

I tried to look back at the leather trunk, what’s covering it is a cravat, with long uneven stitches on it, and you can see how flustered the manufacturer was when weaving this cravat, as I delved deep inside, a dull glow caught my eyes. I stretched my fingers towards it, and it’s a bunch of keys, perhaps from a very ancient time, the rust stains are everywhere on it. After a long scratch of my fingers, the rust scatteredly dropped. But at the thought of that mourning voice, all traces of drowsiness cleared up, and I decided to get dressed to go for some walk.

The night’s clear, like love can be, a wisp of bagpipe music wafting in it. It’s a very familiar melody but I have never heard it before. It’s a feeling like standing on short springy turf near the bank of the river, on a summer’s day, when the slanting rays of sun gilded the ground.

A delicate sugary scent hangs in the air, and the dim trees swaying faintly in the breeze, with its leaves stirring in dense masses like a woman’s hair. And I just stare deep into the limpid, crystal-like river water, until the moonlight gave it a face, it merged with another, another more youthful face. He raised his head and was calling somebody, and another face of a young girl appeared in the water, her eyes piercing the abyss, are so unforgettable. And she reached out her hands to the surface and cupped her hands and scooped up a little water, which made the two faces distorted in the water, and then the two faces vanished into the muffled thunder of hooves.

Antique II Hand Organ

Secretly were these words uttered—I clutched that bunch of keys in my hand; cold, hard, unyielding. Yet I dared not pursue nor satisfy my curiosity beneath my landlord’s roof. But what if I could become a cat? Cats know all things, their grim stares piercing through centuries, slicing through slander and lies. They merely witness, for some fleeting moments, then only comment with a single meow. I wished to be a white cat. But a caterwaul of cats tore me back to the present.

Still, I stood by the riverbank, on springy turf, beneath the sullen moonlight. A pair of small green eyes glinted—eerie emerald, a smoldering gloom in the dark, like embers fading to ash.

That unforgettable gaze was bequeathed from her forebears—older, perhaps, than memory itself. For hundreds, for thousands of years, those little green eyes had been cradled in a princess’s arms, and laid beside a plebeian’s hearth. Always there, to witness, to listen. Her eternal stare held fast, unblinking, etched in the abyss of time; she did not meow, only lifted her head to steal a glimpse, then crept a few steps and looked back—guiding my way.

The jungle was impenetrable; all light seemed swallowed whole. Wind bored into my bones through unseen crevices, thick with the aroma of trampled grass and loam. She ran faster and faster, and so did I. It felt as though I had sprinted for centuries, yet never grew weary. I felt myself growing younger, stronger, with every stride.

I thought dawn must be breaking somewhere, yet impenetrable darkness clung still—deathly silence, save for the jingle and clink of keys in my hand. For a moment, she looked back again. I knew at last where she meant to lead me: right ahead, in the jungle’s deepest heart, lay the wreckage of an ancient manor.

Domination and glory, and empires of a thousand years. All things repeat themselves, yet apocalypse lurks at every breath. No one can guarantee they’ll see the next minute; all fortune is a fleeting gift. Nothing truly belongs to us—and there is nothing she dares not do. The firmament was cloaked, a gloomy net stretched taut, impenetrable. She has bestowed so much hope upon us, so much aid—yet we know only the melody of our own lives, and have never heard another’s tune.

Somehow, it felt like standing on a rock in a river’s center: no way back, no way out. Eyes blindfolded, we read that book, yet not a word do we understand. Its pages turn, and burn. But what else do we care about? We are blind, not deaf—still, we can listen to her melody and dance to our delight, unburdened.

To this very melody, through a million years, she danced for those who could never see, and sang for those who could never hear—following its rhyme, unyielding. I can still hear the cries: the roar of crowds, the din of ancient wars. The lives of others—their apple trees, their fleeting days—pass by, fading, fading into this very moment.

Antique IV Never Been Asked

Even so many years have passed since my quietus, and even after so many times of reincarnation, I can still remember when I saw her for the first time: her flawless hair, her lips, her countenance.

So many mistakes have come to pass, but we can never learn from them. We always love to deceive ourselves into thinking that we’re almost there, even we are still so far, so far away. Though an open field lies in sight, we pretend there’s only one path. And if you think now that this will never happen again, then pay for your naivety. We stay in the narrow gap between, with the equitable summer on one side, making us suppose tomorrow can be the same as today. And with the possibility of being faced with imminent disaster on the other side, which means life can never be like the past.

So many little stories, and so many lessons, happen, happen in here and now, yet can never be understood or learned. So which path will you take? Yet I still believe that she will one day return, even knowing full well, this path leads to the abyss. Once again, we tread on the same path. All the hope being torn and burnt like papers. We always believe that we have so many choices, with so many pages in this book, but it’s doomed that we can only dance on one line of it, pretending, and forgetting, forgiving… no other pages, no other paths can be seen, no other song we can hear. The world we know is painted by her to us. Never been asked, even we have been. Never to be understood.

Are all human relationships just a web of lies? Yet we can never comprehend. The uppermost desire for our species is to be comprehended. We had devoted ourselves to the divine god, forcing ourselves to believe that god is everywhere, he’s always with us and always stands on our sides. What forged our spirit is the god, and only will the god know about its deepest secrets, and we dreamed of a romantic little relationship, persuaded ourselves to believe, if we are fortunate and brave enough, we can find the one, who can know ourselves all, which can break all the seclusion and loneliness, and we immerse ourselves in it, to the idea that we can never be really alone. There’s always one who is always with us. But what we see, what we hear, what we touch, is only the medium we understand the world, and can never really comprehend. So it’s still a lie...

What about we ourselves? We have been an omnipotent deity, but had never been asked, and can never be understood, never be seen, never be heard. There’s nothing we are not able to do, yet nothing we can do, we can only stand by and watch, and only fumbling our own way. I’d rather blindfold my eyes, to pretend that I just can not see! Build a prison myself and imprison myself into it, throw the keys out of the prison and pretend that it had never existed, and forget about the keys, forget about the prison, better forget about the existence of myself. Never ask about it, never question about yourself. Never ask!

It’s her little heart’s last beat, but she’s been ignored so many times, and her problems never interest anyone, never known as the question of all questions. What if you were dead tomorrow? Then I would be alone again. What if you were dead tomorrow? No other soul can hear you cry. Our lungs shared one last breath, I tried to scream, but my lungs just collapsed, my flesh was in you, and my blood was flowing in your veins. It’s the same, exactly the same.

Antique V Seven Tears Across Seven Rivers

For some time, perhaps from a very antique time, I had travelled seven seas, but through the narrow vista of vision, nothing can be seen. What if I had already died seven deaths? In futility, just to forget my name, and forget who I am. A softening inside, suffering, as knowing full well, feeling no remorse. It is said that, of all seven tears, six tears will structure your coffin, and so that your last tear will be squeezed out under the cold deep water, where you’ll finally get your love, but will never be able to know.

But I’m just stuck in here, entangled by water weeds, never flowing to the sea, bound here till the last day of reckoning, on a ruined land. Everyone else, is flowing in the correct direction, leaving only me, encaged here. Time can flow to any direction, while I’ll dream all the misery under the calm water. Only to sing seven hundred songs for the ones who can never hear.

Seven tears, across seven rivers, the dark river, with thyme on their banks stranded high up there. Perhaps, it’ll suffer another journey of seven thousand miles, this terrible journey which no one knows. The seven dark rivers will guide my seven tears to the sea, or perhaps solely a wrong direction, where the deepest ocean runs dry. But will she open her arms to embrace herself? Solely for life.

When the seven tears joined the dark river, and the moonlight bestowed it a countenance again. If you had crossed seven seas, you will hear it, perhaps a sporadic sobbing voice hidden in the thyme, but she had already cried out her seven tears, but encaged in the thyme, solely because this river just leads to an abyss, where her tears can never reach the sea. Or perhaps, when one day, the water will be guided to the ocean after seven million years, where she’ll get the final consolation, but she’ll never know.

In the death‑like silence near the dark river, where only the sporadic sobbing left, if you part the thyme grass, you can see her eyes, flushed like blood, and the tiny hare, under the moonshine, has dreamt for seven lives, for it won’t be long anymore.

Will tomorrow be better? Only knowing full well that in the very end, in the cold dark cemetery, we will lie, and in futility, run in vain for our end, in the everlasting slumber. But just for life. With pity, I mournfully pray to the moon. I wish that, please allow me to become a star on the night sky, every night, still able to shine on her tears.

Antique VII Solace

Black blossoms bloomed upon the river’s surface, keeping time with every periodic beat of her whimpering, sobbing heart. Within those dark petals and the deathly chill of the silvery water, her child was swallowed whole.

She can still remember her smile, an indelible light, as well as the insurmountable poignancy that followed. But there is no way, no choice out of the besieged city; there is only one trench of thorns in sight, and she can only hold her tiny hands to trudge forward. She dares not imagine how these briars lacerate her skin, and the blood…

She never dares to imagine, but chooses to force herself never to turn her head and pretend as if everything is just nonexistent, and as if they have no other choices, as this trail is the only choice, and the only way out—a solace in blindness, which she can still see in the dark.

She remembered holding her for the first time, she was no longer than her forearm, a tiny, exquisite fairy. Her only child, a treasure above all else. She can still recall how she learned to speak, how she laughed, how she played, and how warm it was as she was tucked into her arms when night fell as always-all of them unreeled before her eyes like a haunting tapestry, which fills her heart all day long. Yet blackness and emptiness devoured her from inside, to see her with her own eyes, somehow like an eye in front of the gods. She’s the only creature that she had ever loved; she doesn’t know that she’s already there.

Every morning, she cast cured pork and sourdough rye bread into the current, so that her only child would know no hunger. She still remembers how she watches her do needlework in wide-eyed delight, eyes gleaming as if they had captured all stars in the firmament, the milky way flowing, bringing her the uppermost fulfillment.

She remembers how she shrieked at a needle prick, and her aching heart while seeing red blood permeating her skin; she’s always been so afraid of pain.

Even so many seconds, minutes, and hours, even days, her tiny body has still not been found; her petite fingers are still tangled somewhere beneath the waves. “Away! You devils, begone!” She wished in vain that these water snakes and fish will feast only on her offerings, leaving the delicate body of her child untouched; then she’ll feel no pain on the other side.

And as always, the night falls, the blossom’s red fades black again, like clotted blood. She will tell her stories and sing songs for her at the river bank. The shadow of starlight, soft as a lullaby—she won’t be afraid and won’t feel lonely anymore, never more.

But that is the tender night which her child can never see. She will describe to her how the wind and willow sing, and how stars twinkle. As the moon casts her face to the black water, her only child will know that she’s always there, her countenance embedded and accompany her eternally in the river.

She had never shown her how much she loves her, now she wants to do anything for her. She knits clothes for her and consigns them to the waves; the clothes float and sink so that her only child will never freeze again.

She always imagines that perhaps her heart was too freezing, freezingly cold and converted to a lead weight, suffering her to sink to the deepest water, dragging her to the bottom. And she always fancies if she can melt the ice by showing her love a few days earlier, even only for a single day… She hates God for it.

Did these malicious fish tear at her petite frame? Or did the brutal currents buffet her? Never cared during her lifetime—she hates herself for it.

Another larger blossom bloomed in the river, much more gorgeous. She leaped into the deep ending to give her child a kiss, cradling her devoted child into everlasting sleep. So that in the very end, with her watery accompaniment and embrace, her only child never have to be alone.
