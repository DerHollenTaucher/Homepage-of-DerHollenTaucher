Disgraced

What makes us angry is that we often hold an overly optimistic attitude toward the world and others—an optimism that can reach a dangerous degree. How we react depends on how we define "normal". We might be annoyed by a rainstorm, but if we grow accustomed to storms, a light shower will never upset us. Once we understand our own expectations of the world, and let our experience shape what we consider "normal", we can find peace. We are never angry simply because we do not get what we want; we are angry only when we feel we are entitled to it. Our anger arises when we believe others have violated what we see as the basic rules of life and the world. The world will never be perfect, and we must learn to adapt to it. If we let go of such lofty expectations, we will never be angry again.

Is it strange that a bad person does evil? That your enemies frame you, your friends upset you, and your family makes mistakes—have none of these things ever happened in history?
