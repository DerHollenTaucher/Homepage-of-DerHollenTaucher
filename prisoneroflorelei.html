Sonnet

-('Tis For Thee)-

Still, thou art lief and quaint in my eyne

A gorgeous sight of beauty most divine

As fresh as thyme of May, in sunlight's sheen

With thy clear aura, pure and crystalline

No life have I, but what thy love hath wrought

Still, to this heather，longingly I tether

Rosemary-scented blossom, by nature taught

Foolhardy and tardy I may be, bide together

Avaunt, uncouth and galling blight

Lest a base dastard thou wilt translate

Thy nimbus shines forth, a miht of holy light

Which time nor sorrow ever shall deflate

Sinsyne, let these poor words my boon be

To twain the twine, and set thy spirit free

-(Thy humble child, in everlasting grace.)-

Mirrie Dancers

Hwaet! My dearest, the highest truth I can hold

Is the enchantment how lives unfold

Albeit fading into misery in velvet fright

Thy velvet rancour in twilight

Hither! my precious, weary of this mortal strife

And all the faithless who forsook my life

Though none believe, I bow in gratitude

For through thy wrath alone, I forge my fortitude

One slaughter maketh many appointers

And enraptured by the overlooked time betwixt twin pointers

As different winters have different coldness

So that different dancers, soulless in their blindness

Clothe me in thy velvet rancour, ne’er to rue

Then suffer me to dance with thee, thou pursue

Suffer me to transfix, to exude

Open thine eyne, behold naught save grue

Hark to thy lure of night, echoing hollow meadow

Keeping myself bared under thy shadow

Once black, aathing bloometh anew

While the trail to my citadel, behold nay mo clue
